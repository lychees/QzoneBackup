Hi there,
Mozilla's Summer Code Party is just around the corner, and I can't wait for the kickoff on June 23rd. As part of Mozilla's Hive Learning Network NYC, I helped test the Kitchen Table event concept — and I wanted to let you know how easy and fun it is (and that if I can do it, you can too)!

I knew I was adept at using the Web, but making the Web is still new to me. I'm only just learning the basics of coding, so when I was asked to be a beta-tester for Summer Code Party events, I began playing around with some of Mozillla's tools (which will be made even better with the soon-to-launch Thimble) , checking out Mozilla's Webmaker.org and bouncing ideas off my officemates.

I started out by inviting my family around the kitchen table, and in less than an hour, using only a few simple tools and tutorials, we made some really cool stuff on the Web!

I know it might sound intimidating, but it really is super-simple — plus you'll learn a ton and have a good time in the process. Here's the quick and easy way to host a kitchen table event:

1) Decide on a space & time (it doesn't actually have to be at your kitchen table)
2) Add your event to the calendar at webmaker.org (so you can get your own page to send around to friends, and we can be in touch about ways to help your event go smoothly)
3) Invite your friends
4) Choose a project to work on
5) Start hacking
6) Help each other out
7) Share what you made with the world (we'll help)

It really is that simple — so what do you think? Are you ready to join the party?

Yes, I want to get started hosting an event.

No, but I want to donate to support the Webmaker initiative.

From my mother to my teenage nephews to my young daughter, Mozilla Webmaker gave me the tools and projects that make learning the code behind the Web fun for people, whether they have existing coding skills or are total newbies!

Check out the easy-to-use events guide to get started! And if you feel inspired to throw a bigger party, we've got guides for organizing Hack Jams and Pop Ups, too.

Thanks for being a part of the Summer Code Party!

Lainie

P.S. — I wrote a longer blog post about my experience hosting kitchen table events with a few different audiences — please feel free to check it out . Hopefully it'll help inspire your own events to be even better!
