Q: 为什么你要休学？
So why did you drop out from school?

A:
Well, that is a long story to tell. I'd like to trace back to my middle school year. Since I majored in the Science Development Class (理科实验班) in high school, I dreamed of becoming a scientist one day. And all of my classmates majored in at least one academic competition at that time. "犯其至难、图其致远", which means to challenge the quo, and to explore the unknown territory, was our class motto. I thought that college would become a new stage of my life. But when I entered the university in 2010, the reality was not the same as what I once imagined. Now thinking back, I believe, there are 3 reasons motivating me to make the decision.

  • The first one is, that I found myself lost in the environment. Most of my college classmates scrupled to talk about ideals. They prefered to get a grade as high as possible in every subject they majored in, then take part in the entrance exams for postgraduate schools. That is, in my values, not the entire story of college life.

  • The second is, that few of the curriculums were attractive for me. Some of them had already been learned by myself in middle school, meanwhile some of them like singlechip programming, electric circuit, Marxist theory and military theory, in my humble opinion, are completely useless! Some of them are even wrong! And, unbelievably, most of the curriculums are badly outdated! E.g. the school forced us to use Turbo-C to accomplish the homework, which was beyond my tolerance. In fact, they haven't change the teaching program till now.

  • Most of time, I read in the college library all day before they chase me away. I read everything I can get my hands on, from one shelf to another. Most of them are technology books, and some of others are biographies. There is a book called Hackers & Painters , written by Paul Graham, which inspired me a lot. That is a collection of essays discussing hacking, programming languages, start-up companies, and many other technological issues. I was strongly influenced by the chapter 6 - How to Make Wealth. So I planed to run a start-up from then on, I considered this as the third reason. Coincidentally, at that moment, one of my high school classmates had the same idea. So I dropped out and went to join him.

Q:
你们在创业的时候遇到过哪些困难？
What are the problems you have encountered in your startup?

A:

  • The first problem is - people. We had only a few people at the start, and each one of us had to switch between different status and did multiple tasks simultaneously. So yes, the first priority is always to hire people. But running a small company, with extremely limited fund, we were hard to attract the talent. So the only thing we could do was training the freshmen. This is a trade-off strategy, I mean, it has costed me lots of time. We posted the curriculum schedule on the web board, which was completely free. At first, we only had about 40 students in our class, but soon the situation turned ameliorated. Our project gradually got support from the official college. Our company got valued by the dean's office. We got some money award which substantially relieved our financial pressure. We were even approved to have a huge classroom, as our own office.

  • The second problem is, as I mentioned before, fund. Although we have already got the Angel Capital today, fund is still a problem we need to deal with. Not to mention the beginning. Usually there are two options, either to turn to family members and friends or call for help from the venture capital market. We could not get too much from our parents, after all, these are their pensions. And we have already received money from our founder and friends, e.g., I have alerady invested all the income I got from the mining pool (about 15 thousands RMB) into the business. Although it is still far from enough in nature, so we went to Beijing for venture capital.

Q:
看上去你去过 Google 上海，那里给你留下了怎样的印象？
It seems that you have visited to Google Shanghai, what is your feeling?

A:
Yes, I have visited Google Shanghai several times, and every time it left me unforgettable memories. I remember that once, I accompanied one of my best friends, who had wished to visit Google for a long time. I know she is an artist, but she is quite taciturn. We went to a meeting room, which was amazing designed and just the opposite of what we expected. When we went into that room, we found a book on the desk. That is a design book, to be precise, a book about the room. I couldn't help thinking: Why was it designed in that way, and why it leaves me a deep impression? So, you can see, the architectural designer finished his job, and left a book to share his knowledge. In Google, people are always willing to share both their knowledge and joy, making tools helpful for others, even becoming part of others work. Such an atmosphere can hardly come out from other organizations.
